Research

A Langragian motion budget for High-CWV “Vapor Lakes”

I track multiple water vapor lakes using MERRA-2 reanalysis over the Equatorial Indian Ocean. Despite strong precipitation-driven drying, I am interested in how these lakes are maintained and how they propagate over time. These systems have implications for East African rainfall and also serve as a natural lab for understanding convective–radiative feedback and self-aggregation of convection. I approach this problem from a Lagrangian perspective by linking Eulerian moisture budget terms to the normal motion of the lake boundary, and by quantifying what acts as the driver and what acts as the adjustor of the system. This involves constructing Lagrangian moisture budget diagnostics, separating dynamical, physical, and analysis tendencies, and diagnosing moisture transport pathways using both column-integrated and vertically resolved fields. I am also exploring extensions using M2AMIP and idealized MPAS-based perturbation experiments.

Entrainment and Structure of Tropical Deep Convection

I quantified a bulk entrainment proxy for tropical anvil-forming convective clouds using CloudSat observations. Deep tropical convection plays an important role in transporting heat, moisture, momentum, and trace gases from the lower to upper troposphere and into the stratosphere. Entrainment, or mixing of environmental air into clouds, controls the vertical extent and strength of these systems. I estimate a bulk entrainment proxy using the difference between the observed level of neutral buoyancy (LNB) and that predicted by parcel theory. I then examine its diurnal and seasonal variations over tropical land and ocean. The results show clear differences, with lower entrainment over land and higher entrainment over ocean, highlighting contrasts in convective structure and intensity.
